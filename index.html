The Art

The Artist

Craft rooted in culture,
refined by passion.

"Each design is a conversation between tradition and the moment we share."

I grew up with an innate love for art, and the moment I first picked up a henna cone, I knew this was my language. What began as curious doodles quickly grew into a lifelong passion — and eventually, a craft I share with the world.

I bring a Fine Art approach to mehendi, blending timeless tradition with a refined, personal aesthetic. Every design is bespoke — shaped by your story, your occasion, your vision.

To me, the beauty of mehendi lies not only in the intricate patterns, but in the experience itself — honouring the moment, creating a memory that lasts long after the stain fades.

— Your Artist

Frequently Asked

Everything you need to know before your appointment.

Mehendi typically lasts 5–14 days depending on your skin type, placement, and aftercare. Palms stain the darkest and last longest. Feet and arms may fade sooner.

We use 100% natural, organic henna powder mixed with high-quality essential oils such as eucalyptus or lavender. Our paste is completely free from harmful chemicals or PPD. Your skin's safety is our top priority.

Yes — bridal mehendi is our speciality! A full application covering hands, arms, and feet takes 3–6 hours. We recommend scheduling 2–3 days before your ceremony so the stain reaches its richest colour by your big day.

Absolutely. Every design is bespoke. We can incorporate your partner's name, meaningful motifs, or cultural symbols important to your family. Share your ideas and we'll craft something uniquely yours.

Keep the paste on for at least 6–8 hours (overnight is best). Once removed, avoid washing the area for 24 hours. Apply coconut oil regularly and keep the area warm — heat is the secret to a deep, rich stain.

For bridal bookings, we recommend enquiring at least 3–6 months in advance, especially during peak wedding season. For smaller occasions, 2–4 weeks notice is usually sufficient.
